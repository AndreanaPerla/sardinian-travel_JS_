Cagliari

The Sardinian capital boasts a thousand-year history, clearly visible in its historic districts: Villanova, Marina, Stampace and above all Castello, which dominates the entire city

Orosei

Located in central-eastern Sardinia, the Orosei Gulf is famous for the Bue Marino Caves that overlook a crystalline inlet, and for the presence of some of the most beautiful seascapes on the island.

Maddalena

The Maddalena archipelago has been a Unesco heritage site since 1994. Its 7 islands are the ideal destination for snorkelling enthusiasts due to its pristine seabed and the variety of marine fauna.

Iglesiente

The southwestern area of ​​Sardinia houses ancient mining towns and wild and unspoiled landscapes. Its coast is characterized by high limestone cliffs overlooking the crystal clear sea.

Our destinations

Carloforte

The only town on the island of San Pietro, off the southeastern coast of the island. Dotted with small beaches, the village has a tangle of charming narrow and colorful alleys.

The way of the nuraghi

The itinerary winds through some of the most important examples of Sardinian Nuragic civilization. These include the nuraghe of Palmavera and Barumini, the nuraghe Santu Antine and the nuraghe of Arrubiu.

Caves tour

Sardinia houses spectacular caves scattered throughout the territory. Not only marine (such as the Nettuno and Bue Marino caves) but also caves located in the mountain territory, such as Is Zuddas and Su Marmuri.

Discovering Barbagia

The real heart of the island is located in the province of Nuoro. A wild but at the same time fascinating territory, cradle of millenary traditions. Among the villages touched by the tour, Orgosolo and Mamoiada, home of the Mamuthones.
